No matter who you are or how old you are, this website is for everyone, although our tips are geared towards students from Western University.

    • Study Tips

  • Use active recall!
  • Try the “Brain Dump” method. Take a blank piece of paper and write down as many things (key words, concepts, equations) from your memory!
  • See if you can teach a concept to your friends! If you are able to explain topics to your friends, you most likely know the content.
  • Keep distractions in another room such as your phone.
  • When working on a course, assignment, or just studying, remember to stay diligent. Do not forget to take breaks every thirty minutes.
  • Try to study for each subject everyday for at least 30 mins.
  • Go over notes every weekend.

    Organization Tips

  • Use OneNote to keep all your notes together. You can access your notes on your laptop and your iPad which is super convenient for exam studying!
  • Goodnotes is also really helpful if you're up to spending some money!
  • Have a planner! Write down all your due dates, tests, and meetings in one place right when the school releases these dates. Google calendar is a wonderful way to keep track of all your responsibilities! Physical planners are also great.
  • If you are using a notebook, use sticky notes, highlighters, different colour.
  • Make your notes as pretty as you want, or in anyway that will make you want to study again.

    • Assignment Tips

  • Try to start the assignment when it is given and try to finish it 4-5 days before its due so you have time to check over anything. Make sure to double check your work!!
  • Try to have a couple of people go over the assignment so you can be sure there are no to very little mistakes!!
  • Work very hard on your assignments because they are “practice” for your future exams!

    • Note Taking Tips

  • Annotate slides while your professor speaks! Don't waste time copying down the notes.
  • Take notes on your own time if you really like taking notes.
  • Read and understand the material first. Then take notes from whatever you remember without looking back at the material.

    How to be a better student overall

  • Maintain a good work-life balance! Ensure that you are spending time with your family, friends, hobbies, and more because it is just as important!
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help! Remember, you are in school to learn! So, it's okay if you don't get the concept on your first try!
  • DO NOT PULL ALL NIGHTER!!!!
  • Sleep early and wake up early. This is up to personal preference, but remember you can train your body over time even though you are a night person. If you're going to stay up an extra three hours, you might as well go to bed on time and wake up three hours early instead.
